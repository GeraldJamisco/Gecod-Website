## The Solution: PayPal + Wise (the legal bridge)

Uganda can't withdraw from PayPal directly to a Uganda bank — but **Wise (formerly TransferWise)** solves this completely.

```
Western Donor → PayPal → Wise virtual USD account → Uganda bank account
                                    ↑
                         (Wise provides a real US routing 
                          number that PayPal accepts)
```

**How to set it up:**
1. Create a **Wise business account** at wise.com → it gives you a real US bank account number (routing + account)
2. In PayPal Business settings → link that Wise US account as your bank
3. PayPal thinks it's withdrawing to a US bank → it works
4. Wise receives the USD and transfers to GECOD's Uganda bank in UGX (or holds USD)

Wise supports Uganda withdrawals and is used by thousands of African NGOs and businesses. It's completely legal.

## About the Kenya PayPal idea

I'd advise against it. Opening a PayPal account with a Kenya number for a Uganda-registered NGO is misrepresentation — PayPal can freeze the account and hold the funds if they detect the mismatch. That's a risk you don't want with donor money.
